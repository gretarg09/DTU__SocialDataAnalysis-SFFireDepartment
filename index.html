Fire Department Calls for Service

Analyses of the response time of the San Francisco fire department

The project

Intro
Fire departments play a vital role in most communities over the world. Fire fighter do not only prevent loss of life from fires, their task varies from rescuing victims from hazardous conditions to administering first aid during medial emergencies, in fact that is the biggest part of their jobs. In the article The Importance of a Firefighter they found out that firefighters respond to almost 20 million medical emergencies, compared to approximately 1.4 million fires yearly on a national level in America. The response time of the fire department can make the difference between life and death, it is vital for all communities that the response time is minimized as much as possible.
In this website we will take a look the response time of the Fire Department of San Francisco. The aim of the analysis is to explore the difference in response time in relation to both time and location in San Francisco. More specifically, we will try to create visualization that answers the following questions; is there a difference in response time between neighborhoods in San Francisco? is the response time dependent on the time of the day and is there a difference in response time between different types of calls e.g fire, alarm, life threatening, non life threatening?
To accomplish this we explored the Fire Calls-For-Service data set from San Francisco. This article is devided into the following four sections;
  • INTRODUCTION - Contains the idea of the project and information about the datasets
  • RESPONSE TIME - Responsive histogram of neighborhood's response time
  • GEO PLOT - Responsive geo plot where difference in response time between neighborhoods in San Francisco is visualized geographically.
  • PREDICTION - Machine learning models created in order to predict the response time
To get the most from this website we encourage the reader to go through the website in this order. In this website late responds time is defined as more than ten minutes and very late response time is defined as more than twenty minutes. These numbers are not chosen randomly, according to this article the city standard is that an ambulance should arrive at life-threatening emergencies within 10 minutes from when the call was received. The article also used 20 minutes as the standard for very late responses.

Datasets

The main data set behind the analysis in this website is the Fire Calls-For-Service dataset from the city of San Francisco. The dataset includes all fire units reponses to calls. Each record includes the call number, incident number, address, unit identifier, call type and disposition. All relevent time intervals are also included in the dataset. Because the dataset is based on responses, and since most calls involve multiple units, there are multiple record for each call number. In this article the reponse time is defined as the time that it takes the first responding unit to show up for service. The dataset can be downloaded from following link SF Call For Service . We focused on the period from 1st of January 2012 to 1st of January 2017.

To get more perspective into our analysis we added information about each neighborhood in San Francisco.
